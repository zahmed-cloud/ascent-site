§ The thesis

When outbound underperforms, the cause is rarely a tired channel or burnt-out copy. It is almost always structural misalignment. We design for structure first.

Most teams treat outbound as a pile of tasks spread across people, tools, and time zones. No one person is accountable for the output. We operate the opposite way.

§ 05   Questions

Before the call.

What does Ascent actually do?
Run outbound as a commercial function. Targeting, messaging, LinkedIn and email execution, qualification. One partner. End to end.
Who is it for?
Scaling B2B companies with a proven offer. Founders, agencies, consultants, and SaaS teams with contract values that justify a senior operator running the work. If you are still figuring out what to sell, this is the wrong time for this service.
How is this different from an agency?
Agencies split the work across roles and tools. We do not. One operator is accountable for the pipeline. You are not a line item on a dashboard.
How much does it cost?
Scoped after the first call. Monthly retainer, 90-day minimum. If you are looking for a price list to compare, this is the wrong service.
Do you use AI to write the outreach?
No. AI helps with research and priorities. The words a prospect reads are written by a human for a real buyer.
When do conversations start?
Weeks two to four. Anyone promising booked calls in week one is measuring how easy the target was, not the health of the pipeline.
Can I just buy the list or the copy?
No. Ownership is the product. Selling you a piece of it defeats the point.
