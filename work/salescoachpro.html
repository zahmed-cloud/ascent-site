§ SalesCoachPro · Sales coaching & training

Booking podcast hosts for a sales coaching founder.

Adam needed to reach podcast hosts to grow his audience and his practice. Outbound was eating his time and returning silence. We took it over.

Partner SalesCoachPro
Category Coaching / Training
Channel Email + LinkedIn
Focus Podcast host outreach

01 /The starting point

Adam Pounds runs SalesCoachPro. He coaches founders and sales teams to run better pipeline conversations. His own pipeline was being built by showing up on podcasts — the right ones, with the right hosts, reaching the right founders.

The problem was the outreach to get onto those podcasts. It was piecemeal. He'd send a batch when he had an hour, then nothing for a week. Replies would come in after he'd already moved on to something else. The calendar didn't fill.

02 /What we did

Three things changed:

  • Targeting was rebuilt around podcast fit — audience profile, host role, show cadence, recent guests. Not just "any business podcast."
  • The outreach was rewritten to sound like a human who'd listened to the show, because whoever wrote it had.
  • The replies came to one inbox, handled the same day, by someone who had context.

Nothing fancy. Nothing automated in a way that removed the human. The volume stayed deliberate; the quality went up.

03 /What changed

Adam's inbox started filling with host conversations — real replies, not polite declines. The time he was spending on outbound went from scattered to zero; it moved off his plate completely.

The headline isn't the numbers. The headline is that the work stopped eating a founder's time while improving the quality of the pipeline it was producing. That's the thing agencies rarely deliver, and it's why Adam wrote what he wrote.

04 /Why this worked

The mechanics were boring. Research, write, send, reply. What made it work was that one person held all four. Adam wasn't managing an SDR or pasting notes into a CRM for someone else to follow up on. He saw weekly reporting and took the calls that landed.

It is the same model Ascent runs for every partner: one operator, full ownership, end to end. Podcast outbound is a specific use case. The structure underneath it is general.
